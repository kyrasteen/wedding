bride

kyra weber

I grew up in the small town of Sisters, Oregon. My time spent there, absorbed in the tightly knit community and surrounded by the central Oregon landscape, shaped who I am today. I spent time outdoors and pursuing creative expression. I attended Humboldt State University in northern California for studio art and business. I now live with Christopher in Boulder, Colorado where I am returning to school to study the art of web design and development.

groom

christopher steenbock

Coming back to California to get married feels right -- Kyra and I met while at Humboldt State University and some of my fondest memories are from growing up in southern California's South Bay with a remarkable family and now, clearly, life long friends. Kyra graciously accompanied me to Boulder, Colorado where I am now a PhD student at the University of Colorado. I wouldn't change a thing.

together

We met in an economics class at Humboldt State University. I was studying art and business and Christopher was studying biology and botany. After quite some time, and one coffee date, I asked Chris to go skiing, (and had secretly already rented his gear). After being rained out of the slushy snow, we had a wonderful dinner together. For over six years now we have enjoyed our dinners together. Throughout the rest of our time in northern California, we lived two blocks apart, and shared many memorable times with our friends and roomates. As our new home, Colorado has already shown us beauty in its landscape and kindness in its people. There is an endless amount of adventure to be had here, and we look forward to exploring as husband and wife.

proposal

Christopher has never been one to be passionate about Christmas decorations. I figured he was just excited to use his new saw when he insisted we get a fir tree for our tiny apartment. We drove across a snowy pass to reach forest service land, with cross-country skiis in tow! Christopher had been designing the engagement ring since the past spring with my jewelry friend and mentor Jennifer. I had no idea. He had also told many friends and family that he would propose that day, and even organized a celebration that very evening. Confidence. Once we agreed on a fir, Christopher knelt to the base of the tree as to cut it down, and instead paused to ask me a question.

ladies

Kelly Morton

Beginning at the age of seven, Kelly and I could be found side by side while fishing, hiking, eating, biking, and dancing. Whether in hay, in snow, or in water we have been the closest of friends. Kelly is a beutiful person that perfects the art of a cup of tea, and rides a single speed mountain bike! She lives in Bend, Oregon where she is a Waldorf school teacher. Kelly and her family have always been there for me, and I am grateful that we remain close after 18 years.

Mila Bock

Mila responded to a Craigslist ad for a roommate that I posted while attending college at Humboldt State University. After our initial meeting and a poorly planned canoe trip, we were not only roommates, but close friends. Countless hours were spent with Mila at the kitchen table sipping coffee or beer, while attempting to study. Mila has an admirable sense of adventure, whether scaling the walls of Yosemite with ease, or baking muffins without a recipe, she is quite a lady!

gentlemen
